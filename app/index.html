Deets

The entire week-end, including the wedding ceremony will be very laid back. Swim, go canoeing, play board games, hang out on the dock, nap, tan on the beach, fish and explore surrounding islands.

We are providing food from Friday night to Monday morning. The cottage is on an island and once we are there, we won’t go back on the main land unless there is an emergency so make sure to bring all the extras you’d like to have, including alcohol.

We’ll be serving wine and beer for the dinners but the rest of the week-end will be BYOB.

The cottage will be our main HQ for festivities, eating, and gathering. There is plenty to do around the cottage:

  • Swimming
  • Fishing
  • Long walks
  • Kayak & Canoe
  • Exploring surrounding islands
  • Relaxing, reading
  • Playing games
  • Jumping contests from the dock
  • Late night sauna sessions and midnight skinny dipping
  • Eating marshmallows around the fire

What to bring

Bring your comfy cottage attire, including bathing suit and sunscreen.

It can get a bit chilly during the evenings so make sure to bring pants and sweaters.

Feel free to bring games, fishing rods, swimming gear, hammocks, comic books and other sweet things that you enjoy.

What to wear

Be cute, but casual.

We don’t have a dress code for the wedding ceremony. No tux or super high heels, you’ll sink in the ground anyway and get dirty.

Registry

We do not have a wedding registry but if you’d like to make a contribution, you can make it here at your own discretion, or bring it with you to the wedding.
